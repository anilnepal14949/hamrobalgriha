Introduction

Despite numerous efforts to minimise poverty, Nepal remains one of the poorest countries in the world, with about one-quarter of its population living below the poverty line. Beneath the fact, Nepal being a rich, multi-religious and traditional country, lies a truth of exploitation, social inequity, violence, unjust, discrepancy and a widening gap between the so-called rich and poor. Still, our society is highly patriarchal, hence, women and children are not able to use the available resources in equitable manners and are forced to accept superstitious taboos, subjugation, and evil habits. Starvation and illiteracy are other factors causing more than half of the population to be homeless, beggared and orphans.

Considering all these abovementioned factors, HamroGhar Balgriha, Hetauda (GJSSM Makwanpur) was established on 2008 A.D. as a non-profitable and non-governmental organization to assist the orphan children and women from discriminated communities of Nepal. Our first initiative began in Makwanpur district, supported by Raj Kumar Niraula, sub-chief of district Administration Office by providing food, clothes, housing, and other basic needs, although having limited resources. From its establishment, orphan children and women from different disadvantaged groups, discriminated and poor societies are brought up in a caring and homely environment and providing them with the basic needs such as sound health, home to live in a family environment, good education, balanced diet, skilled and vocational trainings and moreover unconditional love and support to strengthen their beautiful future and potential.

Similarly, children above 18 years of age and these women are provided with various skilled trainings to uplift their lifestyle as well as start their small-scale to large employment services. The development of a country depends on these children and women so its humanity to care for vulnerable and develop them to be a skilled person tomorrow.

We Work To

  • Provide orphans with appropriate meals, sanitary living spaces, and medical treatment.
  • Provide education and life skills to orphans.
  • Develop a community of orphans who are ready to contribute significantly to their society.
  • Offer interventions to help stabilise families that are on the verge of dissolving due to poverty, sickness, or financial collapse, allowing them to stay together.
  • Commit in defending and preserving the rights of all vulner able and underprivileged children, regardless of race, religion, or colour.
  • Produce the highest-quality future leaders who will be responsive, trustworthy, and have a strong desire to serve mankind.
  • Commit to assist the abused, vulnerable, unprivileged, and neglected orphans by standing up for them and bringing out the best in each and every child.
  • Utilise the information acquired from our direct contact with children to advocate for improved childcare policies and promote the rights of all children.
  • With the appropriate treatment and persistent support, the most fragile child’s can be turned around, thus, as a registered non-profit organisation, we are required to be accountable and open in all of our interactions with the government, donor organisations, volunteers, and the general public.

Our Passion

Action

  • To provide quality education, safe and secure home, nutritious food, hygiene, health care and skilled and vocational trainings to orphaned, impoverished, and marginalized children and women of Nepal.
  • To raise the standard of care for orphans; mobilizing a broad network of individuals, organisations, and businesses to meet the physical, emotional, and spiritual needs of orphans.

Accountability

  • Creating and maintaining procedures to determine that orphans receive the best possible care, and that financial assistance is spent wisely.
  • Teaching the children, the ethics of morality, skills of social interaction, respect for people with a sense of community via seminars, consultation, acts, interactions, humanity related programs and other trainings.

Awareness

  • To raise the living conditions of women and children of the disadvantaged and discriminated communities by working towards raising awareness programmes on gender, health, education, human trafficking, drug addiction, violence, HIV/AIDS, child labour, and other cultural and technological advancements.

Development

  • To support sustainable development of women and children by providing them with various employment opportunities and self-development programs.
